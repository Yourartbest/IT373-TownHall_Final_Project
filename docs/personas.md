# User Personas

These personas represent the three core user groups for the Newark AI Town Hall project. They guide decisions in UX, content strategy, information architecture, and workflow automation.

---

# Persona 1 — Newark Parent With Low Tech Literacy
**Name:** Angela Martinez  
**Age:** 38  
**Occupation:** Retail Associate  
**Location:** Newark, NJ  
**Technical Skill:** Low  
**Personality:** Caring, cautious, community-oriented  

## Overview
Angela is a working mother who wants to understand how AI impacts her children’s education. Technology often feels intimidating, so she relies on simple explanations and mobile-friendly interfaces. She values clarity and trustworthiness.

## Goals
- Learn how AI affects education and everyday life.  
- Attend free, beginner-friendly workshops.  
- Understand event relevance quickly.  
- Register easily from her phone.  
- Receive reminders through SMS or WhatsApp.  

## Frustrations
- Technical jargon and confusing explanations.  
- Overwhelming interfaces or too many choices.  
- Difficulty identifying beginner-friendly events.  
- Hesitation asking “basic” questions.

## Needs
- Plain-language content with visuals and examples.  
- Mobile-first layout with large text and intuitive navigation.  
- Clear event labels (Beginner-Friendly, Family-Friendly, Free).  
- One-tap registration and SMS reminders.  

## Digital Behavior
- Mobile-only internet usage.  
- Active on Facebook and WhatsApp.  
- Watches short video explainers.  
- Relies on simple Google searches.

## Emotional Drivers
- Wants to support and protect her children’s future.  
- Fears being left behind technologically.  
- Feels proud when learning something new.

# Persona 2 — Community Educator & School Connector
**Name:** James Thompson  
**Age:** 44  
**Occupation:** Middle School STEM Teacher  
**Location:** Newark, NJ  
**Technical Skill:** Medium  
**Personality:** Analytical, practical, mission-driven  

## Overview
James wants to integrate AI literacy into his curriculum. He needs structured, age-appropriate materials and events he can quickly evaluate. His biggest constraints are time and relevance.

## Goals
- Find AI workshops aligned with student age groups.  
- Access curriculum-ready resources (PDFs, slides, activities).  
- Quickly understand learning outcomes for each event.  
- Share resources easily with colleagues.  
- Connect with other educators in Newark.

## Frustrations
- AI content being too advanced or too generic.  
- Event descriptions lacking specifics.  
- Limited time to sift through disorganized resources.  
- Materials spread across different platforms.

## Needs
- Filterable events (age group, difficulty, topic).  
- Clearly defined learning outcomes.  
- Ready-to-use educational assets.  
- Educator-focused communication channels.

## Digital Behavior
- Heavy use of Google Classroom, Drive, and Gmail.  
- Frequent YouTube searches for quick tutorials.  
- Prefers concise content and scannable pages.

## Emotional Drivers
- Feels responsible for providing equitable tech education.  
- Wants students to feel excited and empowered.  
- Dislikes wasting time with poorly organized information.

# Persona 3 — Student Volunteer & Emerging AI Leader
**Name:** Aisha Rahman  
**Age:** 20  
**Occupation:** NJIT Computer Science Student  
**Location:** Newark / NJIT campus  
**Technical Skill:** High  
**Personality:** Motivated, collaborative, ambitious  

## Overview
Aisha is an aspiring AI leader who wants meaningful volunteer experience, community impact, and resume-building opportunities. She values structure, rapid communication, and collaboration tools.

## Goals
- Volunteer and support Town Hall events.  
- Help organize workshops and manage logistics.  
- Gain real experience using AI tools and automations.  
- Communicate quickly with staff and peers via Discord.  
- Track her contributions for her résumé/LinkedIn.

## Frustrations
- No clear volunteer onboarding workflow.  
- Responsibilities unclear or unassigned.  
- Communication scattered across multiple platforms.  
- No easy way to track volunteer hours or tasks.

## Needs
- A structured volunteer workflow (steps, roles, progress).  
- Discord integration for real-time communication.  
- Event management tools: RSVPs, check-ins, task assignment.  
- Exportable summary of volunteer contributions.

## Digital Behavior
- Daily use of Discord, Notion, Google Calendar.  
- Comfortable with automation and AI assistant tools.  
- Laptop for productivity, mobile for quick checks.  
- Active on LinkedIn & GitHub.

## Emotional Drivers
- Wants meaningful community impact.  
- Values recognition and visible progress.  
- Motivated by teamwork and leadership opportunities.
